## 4. Daily Activities

### Conversation 1: Weekend Plans

**Colleague:** Any plans for the weekend?

**You:** Yes! I'm planning to visit my parents on Saturday. How about you?

**Colleague:** That sounds nice. I'm going to catch up on some reading and maybe watch a movie.

**You:** Which movie are you planning to watch?

**Colleague:** I haven't decided yet. Maybe something on Netflix. Any recommendations?

**You:** Have you seen "The Shawshank Redemption"? It's old but really good.

**Colleague:** I've heard of it but never watched it. I'll add it to my list.

**You:** You won't regret it. It's a classic.

**Colleague:** What about Sunday? What are your plans?

**You:** I'll probably do some laundry, grocery shopping, and meal prep for the week.

**Colleague:** Meal prep again? You're so organized!

**You:** It really helps during the busy week. I don't have to think about what to eat.

**Colleague:** I should start doing that too.

### Conversation 2: Hobby Discussion

**Friend:** What do you do in your free time?

**You:** I've been learning guitar for the past six months. It's really relaxing.

**Friend:** Guitar? That's cool! Are you taking classes?

**You:** No, I'm learning from YouTube tutorials. There are so many free resources.

**Friend:** How often do you practice?

**You:** About 30 minutes every day, usually in the evening after work.

**Friend:** That's dedication. Can you play any songs yet?

**You:** Yes! I can play a few simple songs. Still learning though.

**Friend:** I've always wanted to learn an instrument. Which one would you recommend?

**You:** It depends on what you like. Guitar is good because it's versatile and relatively easy to start with.

**Friend:** Maybe I should try it too.

**You:** You should! It's a great stress reliever and you learn something new.

**Friend:** Do you need an expensive guitar to start?

**You:** No, you can get a decent beginner guitar for ₹5,000-8,000.

**Friend:** That's affordable. I might actually do this!

### Conversation 3: Shopping Trip

**Friend:** Want to go shopping this Saturday?

**You:** Sure! What do you need to buy?

**Friend:** I need some new clothes for work. My wardrobe is getting boring.

**You:** I could use some new shirts too. Which mall should we go to?

**Friend:** How about Phoenix Mall? They have good stores and it's not too crowded.

**You:** Sounds good. What time?

**Friend:** How about 11 AM? We can have lunch there too.

**You:** Perfect! Should we take my car or do you want to drive?

**Friend:** You can drive if you don't mind. I'll pay for parking.

**You:** No problem. I'll pick you up at 10:45.

**Friend:** Great! I'm looking forward to it.

**You:** Me too. It's been a while since we hung out.

**Friend:** I know! Let's make it a fun day.

### Conversation 4: Cooking Experience

**Roommate:** Something smells amazing! What are you cooking?

**You:** I'm trying a new recipe - chicken biryani.

**Roommate:** Homemade biryani? That's ambitious!

**You:** Yeah, I found a recipe online and wanted to try it.

**Roommate:** How long does it take?

**You:** About an hour and a half. I've been cooking for 45 minutes already.

**Roommate:** Do you cook often?

**You:** I try to cook at least three times a week. It's healthier and cheaper than ordering food.

**Roommate:** That's true. I should learn to cook properly.

**You:** It's not that difficult once you start. You just need to follow recipes carefully at first.

**Roommate:** Can you teach me sometime?

**You:** Of course! We can cook together this weekend if you're free.

**Roommate:** That would be great! What should we make?

**You:** Let's start with something simple - maybe pasta or fried rice.

**Roommate:** Sounds perfect. I'm excited to learn!

### Conversation 5: Reading Habits

**Colleague:** I always see you reading during lunch break. What are you reading?

**You:** I'm reading "Atomic Habits" by James Clear. It's about building good habits.

**Colleague:** Is it good?

**You:** Really good! It has practical advice on how to make positive changes in your life.

**Colleague:** I should read more. I used to read a lot but stopped.

**You:** What happened?

**Colleague:** I got busy with work and started watching TV instead. It's easier.

**You:** I understand. I set a goal to read at least 20 pages every day.

**Colleague:** Every day? Don't you get tired?

**You:** Sometimes, but I read before bed. It helps me relax and sleep better.

**Colleague:** That's a good idea. What kind of books do you like?

**You:** Mostly self-improvement and fiction. Sometimes biographies too.

**Colleague:** Can you recommend a good book to start with?

**You:** Try "The Alchemist" by Paulo Coelho. It's short, easy to read, and inspiring.

**Colleague:** I've heard of it. I'll order it today.

**You:** Great! Let me know what you think after you read it.

**You:** Oh, I love the Mission Impossible series! When do you want to go?

**You:** No, but send me the location. I'll find it.

**Friend:** About an hour to an hour and a half. Good workout!

**You:** Perfect. I need the exercise anyway.

**Friend:** Same here! See you Sunday morning then.

**You:** See you! I'm excited.

### Conversation 10: Running Errands

**Roommate:** I'm going out to run some errands. Need anything?

**You:** Where are you going?

**Roommate:** Post office, bank, and then the pharmacy.

**You:** Can you mail this package for me at the post office?

**Roommate:** Sure, no problem. What's in it?

**You:** Just some documents I need to send to my hometown.

**Roommate:** Okay. Do you have the address written on it?

**You:** Yes, everything is ready. Here's ₹100 for postage.

**Roommate:** Thanks. Anything else?

**You:** Actually, can you pick up some medicines from the pharmacy? I'll text you the list.

**Roommate:** Sure, send it over. I'll get them.

**You:** Thank you so much! How long will you be gone?

**Roommate:** Probably about two hours. These things always take longer than expected.

**You:** No rush. I'll be home. Call me if you need anything.

**Roommate:** Will do. See you later!

**You:** See you! Thanks again!
